FAQ

What is Estate Planning?
Estate planning is simply the process of anticipating and arranging for for the disposal of a persons assets. There are many tools we use to create a comprehensive estate plan such as wills, trusts, and powers of attorney.
Why do I need an Estate Plan?
If you die without an estate plan, state law will determine how most of your property is distributed regardless of any of your personal wishes.
What is Probate?
Probate is the general term used to describe the legal process that occurs when someone dies. Probate can be costly and time consuming and one of the major goals of many estate plans is to avoid probate.
How is a Trust different than a Will??
Whereas a Will simply directs the probate court how to distribute assets a trust (geneally) distributes assets without being subject to the probate court.
Why would I need a trust I'm not rich?
You don't have to be rich for a trust to be the right for your estate plan. Trusts can provide much greater flexability than estate plans only contianing a will. Trusts can be especially usefull where one or more benificiaries recieves means-tested governemnt assistance such as Medicid or Supplimental Security Income.
